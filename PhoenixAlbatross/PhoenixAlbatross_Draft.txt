Phoenix-Albatross (draft)

for two dancers, two computer musicians, and a referee (system)


# Competitors

Team X = a dancer and a musician
Team Y = a dancer and a musician


# Game Matrix

	I	II	III	IV	V	VI	0
I	5	-3	5	-3	7	7	3
II	5	-5	5	5	7	-5	-5
III	5	5	-7	5	-7	7	-7
IV	-3	5	-3	-3	7	7	3
V	-7	5	5	-7	7	7	-7
VI	-7	5	5	-7	7	-7	-7
0	5	-7	5	5	-7	7	-7

(Attention!) The first attacker always reads the columns, and the second always reads the rows.


# Tactics

I	= Repeated movement + Repeated sound
II	= Repeated movement + Random sound
III	= Repeated movement + Static sound
IV	= Random movement + Repeated sound
V	= Random movement + Random sound
VI	= Random movement + Static sound
0	= All members pause


# Routing

Dancers wear sensors.
The sensors modulate the sound of the computers.
Musicians transmit their tactics to the referee system simultaneously they perform.


# Referee system

It receives the tactics, calculates the score, and projects the score onto the screen.
A game ends and determines the winner when the first team plays N+1 times and the second team plays N times.
Because although both teams must score the same number of times, the first move does not produce a score.
They will play multiple games to determine the eventual winner.


# How will the teams decide their strategy?

Both dancers and musicians should consider tactics based on the game matrix.
There can be multiple methods.

1. Either the dancer or the musician always decides.
2. The dancer and the musician take turns to decide.

In each case, the dancers and musicians communicate their tactics to their colleagues by hand signs or other means.

3. The dancer and musician perform their respective moves. The musician discerns tactics from the combination of their two moves and sends it. 


# Notes

Ikeda was very interested in Laysan Albatross' courtship dance on YouTube.
Because it looked like the behavior of finite state machines (Meseth 1975).
Ikeda considers Xenakis' "Duel," the original of this work, a humanized finite state machine with a feedback route.
Interestingly, the same algorithm can represent the two extremely different relationships of acts of dueling and courtship.
